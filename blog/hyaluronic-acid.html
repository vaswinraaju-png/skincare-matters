What Is Hyaluronic Acid?

Ingredient Guide • Skin Hydration • Beginner Friendly

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring substance found in the skin that plays a key role in maintaining hydration. It acts as a humectant, meaning it attracts and holds water, helping skin stay plump and comfortable.

Benefits of Hyaluronic Acid

  • Improves skin hydration
  • Reduces dryness and tightness
  • Supports a healthy skin barrier
  • Helps skin appear smoother and plumper

How to Use Hyaluronic Acid

Apply hyaluronic acid on slightly damp skin after cleansing. Follow up with a moisturizer to seal in hydration and prevent moisture loss.

Common Myths

Despite its name, hyaluronic acid is not an exfoliating acid and does not thin or damage the skin. It is gentle and suitable for daily use.

Tip: Hyaluronic acid works best when layered correctly and paired with a moisturizer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use hyaluronic acid every day?

Yes. It is safe for daily use in both morning and evening routines.

Does hyaluronic acid cause acne?

No. It is non-comedogenic and does not clog pores.

Do I need moisturizer after hyaluronic acid?

Yes. Moisturizer helps seal in the hydration attracted by hyaluronic acid.
