What Is Hyaluronic Acid?

Ingredient Guide · Skin Hydration · Beginner Friendly

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring substance found in the skin that plays a crucial role in maintaining hydration. It works as a humectant, meaning it attracts and holds water to keep skin plump and comfortable.

Benefits of Hyaluronic Acid

  • Deeply hydrates the skin
  • Reduces dryness and tightness
  • Supports a healthy skin barrier
  • Helps skin appear smoother and plumper

How to Use Hyaluronic Acid

Apply hyaluronic acid after cleansing while your skin is still slightly damp. Always follow with a moisturizer to seal in hydration and prevent moisture loss.

Common Myths

Despite its name, hyaluronic acid is not an exfoliating acid. It does not thin the skin and is safe for daily use.

Tip: Hyaluronic acid works best when layered correctly and paired with a moisturizer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use hyaluronic acid every day?

Yes. It is gentle and safe for both morning and night routines.

Does hyaluronic acid clog pores?

No. It is non-comedogenic and suitable for acne-prone skin.

Do I need moisturizer after hyaluronic acid?

Yes. Moisturizer helps lock in the hydration attracted by hyaluronic acid.
