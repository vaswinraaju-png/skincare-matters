What Is Hyaluronic Acid?

Ingredient education • Hydration • Beginner friendly

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring molecule found in the skin and connective tissues. Its primary role is to attract and retain moisture, helping the skin stay hydrated, plump, and comfortable.

How Hyaluronic Acid Works

Hyaluronic acid functions as a humectant, meaning it draws water into the skin and holds it there. This improves surface hydration and supports the skin barrier, especially in dry or dehydrated conditions.

Benefits of Hyaluronic Acid

  • Improves skin hydration
  • Reduces the appearance of dehydration lines
  • Supports a healthy skin barrier
  • Lightweight and suitable for most skin types

What Happens When Skin Lacks Hydration

When the skin lacks adequate hydration, it can feel tight, appear dull, and show fine lines more prominently. Sensitivity and irritation may also increase.

Common Myths About Hyaluronic Acid

A common myth is that hyaluronic acid creates moisture. In reality, it holds existing moisture and works best when applied to slightly damp skin and sealed with a moisturizer.

Best Way to Use Hyaluronic Acid

Apply hyaluronic acid after cleansing, while the skin is still slightly damp. Follow with a moisturizer to lock in hydration and prevent moisture loss.

Final Thoughts

Hyaluronic acid is a simple yet effective ingredient for maintaining hydration and comfort. When used correctly and consistently, it can support healthier-looking skin over time.
