What Is Hyaluronic Acid?

Ingredient education • Skin hydration • Beginner friendly

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring molecule found in the skin and connective tissues. Its primary role is to retain moisture and keep the skin hydrated, plump, and comfortable.

How Hyaluronic Acid Helps the Skin

Hyaluronic acid works as a humectant, meaning it attracts water and holds it within the skin. This improves hydration, reduces dehydration-related fine lines, and supports the skin barrier.

What Happens When Skin Lacks Hydration

  • Tight and uncomfortable feeling
  • Dull and rough texture
  • Fine lines become more visible
  • Increased sensitivity

Common Myths

A common misconception is that hyaluronic acid creates moisture. In reality, it holds existing moisture and works best when applied on damp skin.

Best Way to Use Hyaluronic Acid

Apply hyaluronic acid on slightly damp skin and always seal it with a moisturizer. This prevents moisture loss and improves results.

FAQs

Is it safe for daily use?
Yes, it is generally safe for daily use.

Can oily skin use it?
Yes, it is lightweight and non-greasy.

Do I need a moisturizer?
Yes, always seal it with a moisturizer.
