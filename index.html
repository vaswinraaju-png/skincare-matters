What Is Hyaluronic Acid & How It Helps Your Skin

Ingredient education • Skin hydration • Honest skincare

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring molecule found in the skin, joints, and connective tissues. Its primary role is to bind and retain water, helping the skin stay hydrated, plump, and comfortable.

How Hyaluronic Acid Helps the Skin

Hyaluronic acid works as a humectant, meaning it attracts moisture and holds it within the skin. This improves hydration, reduces dehydration-related fine lines, strengthens the skin barrier, and enhances overall skin texture.

What Happens When Skin Lacks Hyaluronic Acid

  • Persistent dryness and tightness
  • Dull, rough skin texture
  • Fine lines become more visible
  • Increased sensitivity

Common Myths & Misconceptions

Myth: Hyaluronic acid creates moisture.
Truth: It retains existing moisture and works best on damp skin.

Myth: Higher concentration works faster.
Truth: Overuse without sealing can worsen dryness.

Best Way to Use Hyaluronic Acid

  1. Cleanse your face
  2. Apply hyaluronic acid on damp skin
  3. Seal with a moisturizer
